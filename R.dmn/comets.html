The Cosmic Snowballs

Comets are icy bodies that release gas or dust. They are often called "dirty snowballs" due to their composition.

Structure of a Comet

Comets consist of a nucleus, coma, and sometimes a tail that points away from the Sun.

  • Nucleus: The solid, central part made of ice and dust.
  • Coma: A cloud of gas and dust that surrounds the nucleus.
  • Tail: Formed when solar radiation pushes dust and gas away from the comet.

A comet displaying its characteristic tail as it approaches the Sun

Famous Comets

  • Halley's Comet: The most famous periodic comet, visible from Earth every 76 years.
  • Comet Hale-Bopp: One of the brightest comets of the 20th century.
  • Comet NEOWISE: A bright comet that was visible to the naked eye in 2020.

Did You Know?

Comets originate from the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud.
